# Candlestick Patterns (Nison + Beyond Candlesticks)

## Core Principles
- **Real body** = open/close range. Shows who WON the session.
- **Shadows (wicks)** = emotional extremes; extraneous noise.
- **Close is king**: a pattern only completes on close; intrabar shapes don't count.
- Reversal pattern ≠ trend reversal — it means the prior trend is CHANGING, not necessarily reversing to the opposite. Could become sideways.
- Always confirm with the prior trend and other technical evidence. A candlestick signal in isolation is weak.

---

## Single-Bar Bullish Signals (after a downtrend / at support)

### Hammer
- Long lower shadow (≥ 2× real body), small real body near the TOP of the range.
- Upper shadow: small or absent.
- Color (white or black) is secondary — position matters more.
- Meaning: bears pushed price down hard, but bulls fought back and closed near the high. "Trying to gauge the depth of the water by feeling for its bottom."
- **Confirmation needed**: next candle closes above hammer's high, or a bullish engulfing follows.
- **Quality boost**: hammer forms exactly at EMA50 / swing support / prior breakout level.

### Inverted Hammer (at bottom of downtrend)
- Long upper shadow, small body near the BOTTOM of range.
- Bulls tried to push higher but failed — yet next day must open higher or close bullishly to confirm.
- Less reliable than hammer; needs strong follow-through.

### Bullish Spinning Top (at support)
- Small real body, roughly equal upper and lower shadows.
- Means: indecision / balance of power shifting. Bulls and bears are at equilibrium.
- Alone is NEUTRAL. Becomes significant inside harami or morning star patterns.

### Doji (at support or after extended move)
- Open = Close (or within 1–2 ticks).
- Represents the most extreme indecision. Bears and bulls exactly tied.
- At the end of a downtrend: **gravestone doji** (long upper wick = bulls tried, failed) is bearish; **dragonfly doji** (long lower wick = bears tried, bulls recovered) is BULLISH.
- After 3+ consecutive bear bars, a doji signals exhaustion. High-probability reversal setup.

## Multi-Bar Bullish Reversal Patterns

### Bullish Engulfing
- Day 1: black (bear) candle.
- Day 2: white (bull) candle that opens BELOW day 1 close and closes ABOVE day 1 open.
- Day 2 body completely engulfs day 1 body.
- Stronger when: day 2 has very long body, high volume, occurs after extended downtrend.
- Al Brooks equivalent: "two-bar reversal" — a large bear bar followed by a large bull bar.

### Piercing Pattern
- Day 1: long black candle (strong bear move).
- Day 2: opens BELOW day 1 low, then closes ABOVE the 50% midpoint of day 1's real body.
- Partial recovery — bulls are gaining ground. Less powerful than engulfing (only >50%, not full engulf).
- If the close is below 50% — NOT a piercing pattern, do not count it.

### Morning Star (3-candle, strongest bullish reversal)
- Day 1: long black candle (confirms downtrend).
- Day 2: small real body (doji or spinning top) that gaps DOWN from day 1 — equilibrium / indecision.
- Day 3: long white candle that closes at least 50% into day 1's black body.
- Meaning: bears were in control (day 1), lost control (day 2), bulls took over (day 3).
- **Morning Doji Star**: day 2 is an exact doji — even more powerful.
- This is the highest-quality 3-candle reversal. Treat it as strong candle confirmation (+1 score).

### Bullish Harami
- Day 1: long candle (bear or bull).
- Day 2: small candle whose entire body is WITHIN day 1's body.
- Means the market has stopped its prior move. Not as strong as engulfing — a "pause" signal.
- **Harami Cross**: day 2 is a doji — more significant.
- Useful as a heads-up but requires 3rd bar confirmation before acting.

### Belt-Hold Line (Bullish)
- A long white candle with no lower shadow (opens on the low, closes near the high).
- At a support area = strong buying pressure from the open.

## What Good Candle Quality Looks Like for Long Entry (Setup A & B)

For the "candle" factor (0–1 point) in the confluence scorecard, award 1 point if ANY of:
1. **Hammer** forming at the EMA50 or retest level (lower shadow ≥ 2× body)
2. **Bullish engulfing** (bull bar body engulfs prior bear bar body)
3. **Morning star** over prior 3 bars
4. **Piercing pattern** (bull bar closes above 50% of prior bear bar)
5. **Doji / spinning top** followed by a strong bull candle (confirmation bar)
6. **Strong bull trend bar**: closes in top 25% of its range, body ≥ 60% of total range, no large upper wick

Award 0 if: indecisive doji without confirmation, small body, large upper wick (distribution signal), or bearish patterns present.

## Bearish Candle Signals — Hard Rejection Triggers

If ANY of these appear at or near the planned entry zone, **reject the setup**:

### Shooting Star / Gravestone Doji
- Long upper shadow (≥ 2× body), small body near the bottom, at a high or resistance area.
- Bears slapped down the bulls. High supply zone.

### Dark Cloud Cover
- Day 1: long white candle.
- Day 2: opens ABOVE day 1 high, then closes BELOW the 50% midpoint of day 1's body.
- Bears overwhelmed buyers. If close is above 50%, it is only a "warning" (spinning top), not dark cloud.

### Evening Star (reverse of morning star)
- Day 1: long white candle.
- Day 2: small body gapping up (bulls losing steam).
- Day 3: long black candle closing ≥ 50% into day 1's body.
- Strong top reversal. If this appeared recently (last 3–5 bars), it invalidates a long setup.

### Bearish Engulfing
- Bull candle followed by a bear candle that completely engulfs it.
- Strong selling. Reject any long in the same zone unless price has recovered with strong volume.

## Nison's Key Maxims for Our System

1. "An important principle is to place a new position based on a reversal signal ONLY if that signal is in the direction of the major trend." → We only take longs in confirmed uptrends (4h EMA50 > EMA200).
2. "Candlestick patterns should always be viewed in the context of what occurred before." → The candle is the final confirmation, not the reason to trade.
3. Close above/below a level is required to confirm; intrabar breaks don't count.
4. Long white real body bodies become SUPPORT on pullbacks — midpoint and bottom of wick.
5. A long white candle followed by small real bodies = buying pressure weakening. Be cautious.
