# Price Action Principles (Al Brooks + Reminiscences of a Stock Operator)

## Foundational Insight
"90% of all trading in large markets is done by institutions. The market is simply a collection of institutions — almost all profitable over time. Every trade has a profitable institutional trader taking the other side." (Brooks)

→ **Implication**: When you see a clean breakout or pullback, institutions have already positioned there. You are joining an institutional wave, not predicting. Trade what you SEE, not what you expect.

---

## Trend Analysis — Brooks Framework

### A Strong Trend Has These Properties
- Bars with large bull bodies, little overlap between successive bars.
- Pullbacks are shallow (< 50% retracement of the prior swing).
- Each new high exceeds the previous high; each pullback low is higher than the prior pullback low (higher highs / higher lows).
- Price stays above the 20 EMA (or barely touches it, then bounces).
- Volume expands on impulse legs, contracts on pullbacks.

### Warning Signs that a Trend Is Weakening
- Large bear bars appearing in a bull trend (institutional selling pressure accumulating).
- Consecutive dojis / spinning tops = momentum bleeding out.
- A "sell climax": after 30+ bars without a meaningful pullback, a suddenly LARGE bear bar. This exhausts sellers and attracts buyers. Often marks at least a 2-leg correction.
- Pullbacks starting to retrace > 62% of the prior impulse.
- 3 pushes higher with each push smaller ("wedge" or "buying climax") → beware.

### The 75% Rule (Brooks)
"The odds that a pullback will get back to the prior trend's extreme fall substantially if the pullback retraces 75% or more."
→ If a pullback corrects > 75% of the prior bull swing, treat it as a potential new bear move, not a buying dip. This is an implicit reject condition for Setup A.

## Entry Signal Bar Quality (Brooks)

A good signal bar for a long entry:
- **Bull trend bar**: opens near low, closes near high, body ≥ 60% of range.
- No large upper wick (distribution signal = institutional selling above).
- Ideally closes in the top 25% of its range.
- Volume is at least average or expanding.

Poor signal bars to avoid:
- **Doji**: equal upper and lower wick. Alone = indecision. Wait for the NEXT bar to confirm direction.
- **Bear bar as the entry bar**: even if it forms at support, entering on a bear close means you're fighting momentum. Wait for the next bullish close.
- **Large upper wick / shooting star**: price rejected higher levels. Supply above.

### Two-Bar Reversal (Brooks)
- 1 large bear bar + 1 large bull bar immediately after = strong institutional reversal signal.
- The bull bar should: open near or below prior close, close near or above prior bar's open.
- Enter on the CLOSE of the bull bar or at one tick above its high.
- Stop: below the low of the bear bar.

## Breakout Quality Criteria (Setup B Enhanced)

A genuine breakout vs. a fake breakout — how to tell:
1. **Genuine**: 2–3 strong bull bars after the range high break, bars don't overlap much, volume spikes.
2. **Failed / trap**: breakout bar is large but the next bar reverses almost entirely, or it closes below the breakout level. This is a "bull trap" — institutions sold into the excitement.

**Key rule (Brooks)**: "One of the most important skills is the ability to reliably distinguish between a successful and a failed breakout. Will it lead to a swing in the direction of the breakout, or in the opposite direction?"

For us (Setup B):
- Require volume ≥ 1.3× avg (screener) AND ≥ 1.5× avg in the breakout bar itself (Claude check).
- Prefer a RETEST of the broken level over chasing the first breakout bar.
- The retest bar should be a small bear bar or doji that does NOT close back below the range high.

## Pullback Entry Quality Criteria (Setup A Enhanced)

Types of pullbacks that are HIGH QUALITY (institutional accumulation):

### "High 2" Pattern (Brooks)
- In a bull trend, price makes a small pullback (leg 1), bounces weakly, then makes a second pullback leg slightly lower (leg 2) = "two-legged pullback."
- The second leg creates a "higher low" relative to a bigger swing.
- Enter on the close of a bull bar at the end of leg 2, or on a break above that bar's high.
- This is the textbook Setup A entry.

### "ii" Pattern (inside-inside)
- Two consecutive bars where each bar is inside the prior bar.
- Represents compression before a directional move.
- Enter on the breakout of the second inside bar (above its high for longs).
- Stop: below the low of the first inside bar.

### Single Inside Bar at EMA50
- A small bar (narrow range) where price dips to or through EMA50 but closes above it.
- Next bar breaks the inside bar's high = entry.
- Stop: below the inside bar's low or the EMA50, whichever is lower.

## Al Brooks on Pullback Depths
- **Shallow pullback (25–38%)**: strongest signal — big money is buying aggressively.
- **Normal pullback (38–62%)**: ideal Setup A zone. Healthy retracement.
- **Deep pullback (62–75%)**: still valid but trend is under more stress. Require extra confirmation.
- **> 75% pullback**: treat as potential trend failure. Significantly lower probability. Requires 10/10 confluence to consider.

## Reminiscences of a Stock Operator — Livermore's Principles

**"The line of least resistance"**: In a bull trend, the path of least resistance is up. Trade with it, not against it. Don't try to pick tops or bottoms.

**"Big money is made by sitting, not trading"**: Once in a valid trend trade, sit. Don't exit at the first sign of wobble. Let the market prove you wrong with a close below your stop.

**"Wait for the market to confirm your idea"**: Don't enter until the market moves in your direction first. For us: wait for the signal bar to CLOSE bullishly, not just intrabar.

**"Markets are never wrong; opinions often are"**: If the price action contradicts your analysis, the analysis is wrong. Reject the setup. Do NOT force a signal.

**Timing**: Livermore said he was right about markets but lost money by acting too early. For crypto: the setup must be FULLY formed. No anticipation entries on incomplete patterns.

## Institutional Behavior Context (Brooks)

"When you buy at a 2-legged pullback, you are entering where the strong institutions entered — not ahead of them."

Key implications:
- **Support levels that hold across multiple timeframes are more reliable** because more institutions are aware of them.
- When price returns to the midpoint of a prior long white candle (Nison), institutions who bought there will defend it.
- The more times a level is tested WITHOUT BREAKING, the stronger it is — but eventually it WILL break if tested too many times. By the 4th–5th test, it weakens.

## Market Regime Identification (Brooks + Reminiscences)

| Regime | Characteristics | What to do |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| **Strong bull trend** | Large bull bars, shallow pullbacks, EMA50 sloping up, price stays above EMA200 | Setup A pullbacks — HIGH priority |
| **Bull trend with pullbacks** | Bull bars, but some bear bars mixed in, price dips to EMA50 | Setup A — requires strong candle confirmation |
| **Range / consolidation** | Flat EMAs, overlapping bars, price oscillates | Setup B ONLY on confirmed breakout from range high |
| **Chop / high volatility** | Large wicks, bars overlap > 50%, no clear structure | NOTHING — reject everything |
| **Downtrend** | Lower highs/lows, price below EMA200, EMA50 < EMA200 | No longs. Hard rejection. |

"High volatility chop: huge wicks, ATR spiking, no structure — NOTHING is allowed." (strategy.md)

## Sell Climax — Special Case (Brooks)

After a decline of 30+ bars with no meaningful bounce:
1. Sellers become exhausted.
2. A SUDDENLY LARGE bear bar forms (institutions step aside briefly = "sell vacuum").
3. Both bulls AND bears buy into that large bear bar: bears cover shorts, bulls initiate longs.
4. Result: sharp 2-leg bounce to at least the 20 EMA.

**For our system**: if a symbol has just experienced a sell climax and is now in the first 2-leg bounce, a Setup A at the EMA50 is ESPECIALLY high quality. Note it in reasoning.

Climax signals to look for:
- 15+ consecutive red bars on the 1h chart.
- Last 3 bars: each larger than the previous (acceleration into climax).
- RSI < 25 (extreme oversold).
- The reversal bar: a hammer or bullish engulfing after the climax bar.
